# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Fans of indie and underground music. Vinyl collectors who follow niche artists, browse Bandcamp, and care about their musical identity. They use this to materialize that identity as a shareable artifact — not to celebrate going viral, but to document taste.

## Product Purpose

A Spotify-integrated fan ticket generator. Users sign in with Spotify, select an artist, and receive a downloadable ticket showing their top tracks and artist data. The ticket is the product — a physical-feeling artifact of musical identity.

## Brand Personality

Vintage, editorial, underground. The aesthetic of a record shop zine: paper textures, ink-weight typography, collector-grade density. Musix Records.

## Anti-references

- Spotify Wrapped: festive, animated, mainstream streaming aesthetic
- Glassmorphism-heavy UI: decorative blurs, neon gradients
- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient hero, floating cards, "modern" sans-serif monotony
- Apple Music Replay: polished consumer tech, too clean

## Design Principles

1. **The ticket is the artifact.** Every screen exists to deliver the ticket. Design decisions should serve the final downloadable object.
2. **Collector depth over simplicity.** Show rich data — followers, genre, track details. Trust users to appreciate density; they came for it.
3. **Material over digital.** Paper, ink, vinyl. The interface should feel tactile, like print. Avoid anything that reads as "app UI."
4. **Underground credibility.** Avoid the mainstream streaming look. The identity here is Bandcamp and Discogs, not Spotify or Apple.
5. **Identity over celebration.** This is about expressing a musical self, not sharing a moment. Tone is assured, not festive.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Sufficient contrast, keyboard navigation, correct semantic HTML. No specific reduced-motion requirements flagged, but honor prefers-reduced-motion for any animations.
